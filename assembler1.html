On a computer we can easily "assemble" and model molecular cages of different size and shape before we make them in the lab. In this way we can quickly predict if a molecular cage could work well for a specific application.

We will now help you in bulding a molecular cage!
Imagine that building a molecular cage is like building a lego structure. Molecular precursors are our lego blocks.
In the next steps 2 different types of lego blocks will be defined: Building Blocks (colored in red) and Linkers (colored in blue). But first, in order to build a molecular cage you need to select a shape, which represents the framework used to correctly position the Building Blocks and Linkers. The Building Blocks will replace the grey spheres in the shape, while the linkers the grey lines.

Larger molecular shapes will generate more flexible cages. More flexible cages will have a higher chance of not being "shape persistent", meaning they can collapse and lose their internal pore. This means that the cage will not be porous.
